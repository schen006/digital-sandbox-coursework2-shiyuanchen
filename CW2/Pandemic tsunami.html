Background of Delmicron

January 01, 2022

A new super-variant could be created if Omicron and Delta infect someone at the same time, a possibility highlighted by Paul Burton , chief medical officer at Modena. Although some researchers immediately pointed out that the new coronavirus evolved by mutating to produce new variants, not by swapping genes between viruses. But this still ignited a public panic and caused a big twitter debate, so let's now take a look at the interesting data.

Twitter data Analysis

The report analyzed tweets about #Delmicron in detail. The tweets obtained were collected using TAGS V6.1, and the time span was from 14:47pm on December 27, 2021 to 23:59pm on January 4, 2022. Of the 18,415 tweets collected during the week, 81.53% were retweeted. However, only 4,172 tweets accounted for 18.47 percent of the total.

The overall trend

In the bar chart below we can see the change in the number of tweets discussing delmicron over a 9 day period. 27th and 28th were the peak of discussion and then it started to fall off a cliff.

TWEETS PER MINUTE

This line chart shows the number of tweets per minute from 3:00pm to 6:00pm on the 27th, during the peak of the discussion on delmicron. It is easy to see that the maximum number of tweets per minute is 44 and the minimum is 15. The average is about 27 tweets per minute.


TWEET TO RETWEET PER MINUTE

The line chat below further compares the number of tweet retweets and original tweets from 3:00 to 6:00 p.m. on the 27th. The plurality of tweets per minute is 21; the overall average is about 20; the median is 20. The plurality of original tweets is 7; the overall average is about 7.3; the median is 7. From this we can see that there is an overall positive correlation between tweet retweet rate and original tweet rate, although there are occasional exceptions. At 16:11 the retweet rate reaches 39 tweets per minute, with an average of 1.53 seconds before a tweet about delmicron is retweeted, while the original tweet figure is at its lowest, with an average of 12 seconds before an original tweet is sent, showing a negative correlation.


Of all the retweets, the tweet about delmicron by tweeter Ali Osman Önder reached the highest number of retweets at 682 and received 2,875 likes. The blogger is a journalist and writer from Istanbul, Turkey, and currently has 65.4k followers. It's worth mentioning that he published three other tweets that also squeezed into the #2 and #3 and #6 spots for the highest tweets. (Statistics as of 15 January 2022)

Demographic Grouping

After taking a look at some of the detailed tweeting data of the 10 users above, let's analyse the characteristics of the followers and friends of all Twitter users for the entire period from 27 December to 4 January. From the pie charts below, we can clearly see a pyramidal structure, with 67.36% and 66.47% of users having a follower and friend count between 1 and 500, which is the first tier at the bottom, and the second tier with 27.55% and 29.18% of users having a follower count between 500 and 1000 respectively. The 3rd gradient is the number of fans and friends above 50k, the number that can be reached is then very small but they still account for 4.38% and 2.91% respectively, and from this gradient onwards there is only one person, the official account @ABPNews with 10 million followers. There are also a few users with blank in the number of friends and followers, who also account for 0.68% and 1.43% respectively.

Analyzing it in more detail, I found that at the first level interval, because of the large data base, the total number of followers counted from 1 to 500 is 100% coherent. By that I mean pick any number from 1-500 and I will find the followers count for that. One step up the ladder, the coherence is not so strong. Follower counts ending in 5 and 7 are the most likely to be missing. By the third gradient, the follower data is highly unique and no one has the same number of followers anymore.


Of all of them, one with the id @atabenli is a person attracts my particularly attention. This is because he has more than a million Followers and Friends. In particular he has 1.2M followers, second only to official accounts like @IndiaToday and @IndiaTodayFLASH. He joined Twitter in May 2011 from Istanbul, Turkey, where he used to work as an interior designer, and currently has a total of 61.2k tweets.
But one thing need to think about is that as of 16 January 2022, his follower count was 1294690, 215377 more than the 1079313 counted on 28 December 2021; he has grown by an average of about 10256 followers per day in the last 20 days. Despite the high follower count and the rapid growth in followers each day, most of his tweets only garner an average of 300-400 likes and ten digit or single digit comments. It is has to suspect that most of his followers are all bots.

DISCOURSE

Obviously, from the chart below we can see that among all the words Turkish is the most dominant language, followed by English. The hashtag #Omicron, #delta, #Covid, #Covid_19, etc. also appear in the tweets along with #delmicron, so they make up a large part of the tweets. In addition, a small number of users use hashtags such as #HappyNewYear2022, #HappyNewYear, etc. Besides, the second most frequent words are mainly Turkish words 'varyant', 'delmicronrt', 'sehir', 'yeni', 'daha', 'olur', etc. Their English meanings are 'variant', 'delmicron', 'city', 'The new', 'more and more', 'possible'.

Conclusion

With this data analysis, it's easy to see that the #delmicron topic is being discussed mainly in Turkey. As of January 17, 2022, people are no longer interested in the topic and delmicron is gradually fading out of the limelight. In any case, let's hope that the epidemic will soon be over and we will have a tomorrow free from worry.
